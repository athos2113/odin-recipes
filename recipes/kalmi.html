Kalmi Kebab

How to make Kalmi Kebab

Kebabs or commonly known as kabobs has its origin in Middle Eastern cuisine and are widely popular across the world. You can easily find the mention of kebabs in Indian and Pakistani cuisine, as they are highly influenced from Mughlai cuisine, which had several kebabs. In India, if you are in Lucknow, it would be easy for you to find several types of kebabs, and mind you, there are many that you might not have heard of!

Ingredients

  • 3 cup chicken
  • 2 teaspoon ginger
  • 3 clove
  • 3 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 3 strand saffron
  • 1 teaspoon powdered cinnamon
  • 2 onion
  • 2 lemon wedges

Recipe instructions (taken from the book)

  1. Cut the chicken into cubes and soak in hot water for 2-3 minutes.
  2. Dry the chicken pieces and add salt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, 2 clove buds and cinnamon powder.
  3. Mix together all the ingredients and then add all purpose flour, eggs and chopped onions in it.
  4. Mix well once again. Let the chicken marinated for 30-40 minutes.
  5. Arrange the chicken pieces in skewers and put these skewers on a griller.
  6. Preheat the griller at 350 degrees and place the skewers on it and grill the chicken for about 8-10 minutes,.
  7. Pour a little lemon juice over the chicken for extra flavour.
  8. Once the chicken cubes are grilled, add them in a pan and saute for 2-3 minutes for a better flavour.
